Overview

Breaking Bad is an American neo-Western crime drama television series created and produced by Vince Gilligan. The show follows Walter White, a high school chemistry teacher diagnosed with inoperable lung cancer who turns to manufacturing and selling methamphetamine to secure his family's financial future.

Alongside his former student Jesse Pinkman, Walter enters the dangerous world of drug trafficking, evolving from a sympathetic family man to a ruthless criminal mastermind known as "Heisenberg."

The series aired on AMC from January 20, 2008, to September 29, 2013, spanning five seasons and 62 episodes. It received widespread critical acclaim and is often cited as one of the greatest television series of all time.
